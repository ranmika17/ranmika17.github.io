My Life with Cricket and other Sports

Published on Jan 1, 2025 • 5 min read

🏏Cricket is one of my favourite things to talk about. I love that game so much. But why did I leave my team then? I am going to reveal the real reason for leaving my team behind.

🔥 How good was I really?

I was a npc in my team back then. I didn’t care at first,⭕ because I just played for fun.😗 Later I wanted RISE. I worked hard and got selected to team B first. I was better than some GOOFY Team B teammates,😁 but Team A was on another level.💥💯 Later I joined Team A. I think I was good at cricket.😈 I rate myself 7/10.🙇‍♂️🤷‍♀️ The reason I left cricket was to focus more on STUDY. I had to raise my grades as I couldn’t improve them anymore.🤣 Not funny tho😑👊

🏋️‍♂️ Training & Discipline

After joining Team A, I realized that talent alone wasn’t enough. I had to train harder than ever.💪 Early mornings, long practices, and strict routines became my life. Some days I felt exhausted 😓, but the thrill of improvement kept me going. Discipline taught me patience and consistency — skills that later helped me not just in cricket but in studies and life in general.

🤝 Team Dynamics

Being in Team A was a different world. Everyone had skills, but teamwork was key. I learned that communication, support, and understanding your teammates’ strengths were just as important as personal talent. I also learned how to handle competition. Not everyone is your friend in sports, but respect goes a long way. 🏏✨

🎯 Lessons Learned

  • Hard work beats talent when talent doesn’t work hard.
  • Never underestimate the power of consistency.
  • Team spirit and collaboration are essential.
  • Balancing studies and sports is tough but necessary.
  • Failures are stepping stones, not setbacks.

💡 Reflections

Looking back, leaving my old team was a tough choice, but it led me to better opportunities and growth. Cricket taught me life lessons I couldn’t learn anywhere else. It wasn’t just about scoring runs or winning matches — it was about discipline, passion, and perseverance.🔥

🚀 Moving Forward

Even though I don’t play competitively anymore, cricket remains a huge part of who I am. I carry the lessons, the memories, and the friendships I made on and off the field. And yes, I still watch matches, cheer for my favorite teams, and dream of one day coaching or mentoring young talent. 🙌
